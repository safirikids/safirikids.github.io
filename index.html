Who it's for

One quiet channel for everyone who cares about that small human.

For parents

Know where your child is.

Watch the bus on a live map. Get a soft chime when it's two stops away. See a verified photo when your child is dropped off.

  • Live map with the bus, your stop, and ETA
  • Calm notifications: ‘two stops away’, ‘at school’
  • Pick-up codes and verified drop-off photos
  • Direct line to the driver, on the school's terms
For drivers

Less juggling, more driving.

One screen. Today's manifest. A green tap when a child boards, a yellow tap when they leave. No spreadsheets, no group chats.

  • Today's manifest, route and stop sequence
  • One-tap boarding & alighting, with parent in the loop
  • Hands-free voice notes for the school
  • Works through a tunnel — syncs when back
For schools

Transport that runs itself.

Routes, drivers, students, fees — in one place. Replace WhatsApp chaos with a quiet, auditable channel.

  • Routes, vehicles, drivers and assignments
  • Per-term fees, invoices, and payouts to drivers
  • Audit trail: every boarding, every drop-off
  • Roll over a new term in minutes, not days
A morning, in three taps

How a Tuesday actually goes.

No app teaches you anxiety. The point of Safirikids is to disappear into the background of your morning, until it has something useful to say.

  1. 1

    06:42 — bus pulls onto your street

    Your phone gives one soft chime: ‘two stops away’. You finish the toast.

    Two stops away
  2. 2

    06:50 — boarding

    Driver taps Aisha's name. Her parents see ‘boarded · seat 12’. The map turns sage green.

    Boarded · seat 12
  3. 3

    07:18 — at school

    Geofence at the gate auto-confirms arrival. Photo of class line-up at 07:22.

    Arrived · St. Mary's
  4. 4

    15:15 — pick-up code

    School issues a fresh 4-digit pick-up code. Driver and parent both see it.

    Code 7-3-9-1
  5. 5

    15:58 — home

    Driver taps ‘delivered’. Photo of Aisha at the gate, signed by the guard. Day closed.

    Delivered · home
